Athletic background


Ever since I was young my dream was to play professional sports. It didn't matter what sport it was, I was determined to make it to the pros.

The list of sports I played growing up consist of:

  • Football: 11 years
  • Baseball: 10 years
  • Basketball: 6 years
  • Track: 2 years
  • Soccer: 1 year

In high school, however, I gravitated to playing football, earning All-State honors and playing in 2 state championships. In college, I went from being a Walk-On bench player, to a starting wide receiver, a senior captain, and ultimately an invitee to an NFL Rookie Mini-Camp.

Regardless of the sport, I truly believe that my involvement in sports helped me become a better student, as well as a better employee in the professional setting.

Lessons that sports teach


Teamwork Perseverance Competition
The ability to work cohesively with others is a key part of playing team sports. A team's ability to mesh is directly correlated to their success. At a young age, I learned how to be a good leader, how to encourage and motivate others. Being open to criticism from your peers is also a key component of teamwork. Being able to identify individual strengths and synergies among the team members is also crucial for team success. No one likes to fail, but building the strength and the courage to keep trying, while understanding the possibility of future failure, is crucial for learning a new skill, on or off the field. As a professor of mine says, "in order to learn, you must have to suffer a little bit." I love competition and what you can learn from yourself in those situations. Being able to compete, both with yourself and with others, will push the boundaries of your performance. Knowing how to keep competition healthy is important, since it can be easy to be discouraged when you lose. But being a competitor is extremely important in many aspects of life.
